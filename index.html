Welcome to the Zoo

The Zoo has over 100 different species. Here is a list of our most popular animals:

Here are some intersting facts about me:

  • Koalas can sleep up to 18 hours a day.
  • Koalas aren’t bears – they’re marsupials!
  • Koalas can be found in southeastern and eastern Australia.
  • Sadly, koala numbers are on the decline.
  • ‘Koala’ is thought to mean ‘no drink’ in the Australian Aboriginal language.

Here are some intersting facts about me:

  • Elephants are the world's largest land animal.
  • Elephants spend between 12 to 18 hours every single day!
  • Their tusks are actually teeth.
  • They communicate through vibrations.
  • Calves can stand within 20 minutes of birth.

Here are some intersting facts about me:

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  • Prehistoric pandas lived up to 2 million years ago.

Here are some intersting facts about me:

  • Giraffes are the tallest mammals on Earth.
  • They can run as fast as 35 miles an hour over short distances
  • Giraffes only need to drink once every few days.
  • Giraffes spend most of their lives standing up; they even sleep and give birth standing up
  • A giraffe’s spots are much like human fingerprints. No two individual giraffes have exactly the same pattern.
